Cristiano Ronaldo

Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro was born on February 5, 1985, in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal, is widely regarded as one of the greatest football players of all time. Known for his incredible athleticism, technical skills, and prolific goal-scoring ability, Ronaldo has achieved success at both the club and international levels.

He is the youngest of four children. His mother, Maria Dolores dos Santos Aveiro, and his father, José Dinis Aveiro, played significant roles in his early life. His father passed away in 2005 due to alcoholism-related issues.

Euro

Cristiano Ronaldo's career in the UEFA European Championship (Euro) is marked by remarkable achievements and memorable performances. Here is a brief overview of his Euro career and his notable accomplishments

Euro 2004


Cristiano Ronaldo made his Euro debut in 2004 as an 18-year-old.
Performance: He scored his first Euro goal in the group stage against Greece and added another in the semi-finals against the Netherlands.
Portugal's Finish: Portugal reached the final but lost to Greece 1-0.instagram

Euro 2012


Performance: Ronaldo scored twice against Hungary in the group stage and once in the semi-final against Wales.
Historic Achievement: Portugal won the Euro 2016 title, their first major international trophy. Ronaldo, despite being injured in the final against France, provided leadership from the sidelines.
Personal Achievement: With this win, Ronaldo became a European champion at the national team level.

Euro 2016


Performance: Ronaldo was instrumental in Portugal's run, scoring twice against the Netherlands in the group stage and the winning goal against the Czech Republic in the quarter-finals. Portugal's
Finish: Portugal reached the semi-finals but was eliminated by Spain in a penalty shootout.

Euro 2020


Performance: Ronaldo was in exceptional form, scoring five goals in the tournament and providing one assist. He scored twice against Hungary and twice against France in the group stage, and once against Germany.
Portugal's Finish: Portugal was eliminated in the Round of 16 by Belgium.
Personal Achievement: Ronaldo finished as the tournament's top scorer (shared with Patrik Schick of the Czech Republic) and became the all-time top scorer in European Championship history with 14 goals.

Unknown Facts about Ronaldo

Name Origin
Ronaldo was named after Ronald Reagan, who was the U.S. President at the time of his birth and one of his father's favorite actors.
Heart Surgery
As a teenager, Ronaldo underwent heart surgery to address a racing heart condition. The procedure was successful, and he returned to training a few days late.
Language Skills
Ronaldo speaks Portuguese, Spanish, and English fluently. He has also picked up some Italian during his time at Juventus.
